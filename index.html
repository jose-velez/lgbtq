Education should be the first priority of individuals when trying to understand something. Not educating ourselves could lead to misinformation. Spreading misinformation could lead to erroneous ways of understanding something.

Knowing what the acronym LGBTQIA+ means is very important since it involves an individual’s gender identity and/or sexual orientation. Being an ally to the LGBTQIA+ community starts by being well educated and understanding everything related to the community.

I’m going to point out something from an academic resource to show the difference between hermaphrodite and intersex.

“In biology, “hermaphrodite” means an organism that has both “male” and “female” sets of reproductive organs (like snails). In humans, there are no “hermaphrodites” in this sense, although doctors have called people with intersex conditions “hermaphrodites.” We find the word “hermaphrodite” misleading, mythologizing, and stigmatizing. Although some intersex activists do reclaim and use this term to describe themselves, it is not an appropriate term to refer to intersex people in general. In short, snails are the hermaphrodites; humans are not. Also, please avoid using the word “intersexual” as a noun; we prefer “intersex people” or “people with intersex conditions/experiences” (Intersex Initiative).

There are a few more terminologies to keep in mind. According to Asexuality 101:

  • Aromantic – people who experience little or no romantic attraction and are content with close friendships and other non-romantic relationships.
  • Demisexual – people who only experience sexual attraction once they form a strong emotional connection with the person.
  • Grey-A – people who identify somewhere between sexual and asexual on the sexuality spectrum.
  • Queerplatonic – One type of non-romantic relationship where there is an intense emotional connection going beyond what is traditionally thought of as friendship.
  • Romantic orientations – Aces commonly use hetero-, homo-, bi-, and pan- in front of the word romantic to describe who they experience romantic attraction to.

If you have not seen some of these terms before, now is a great opportunity to familiarize yourself and try to remember them! The LGBT+ community really needs help and allies since the amount of rejection and oppression they receive is extremely high.

Ways to support the LGBTQIA+ community

  • Talk to family and friends.
  • Take schoolwork/meeting opportunities to educate others.
  • Spread reliable information through social media.
  • Join clubs or organizations in your community.
  • Be creative and help educate others.
  • Try to reach law makers in order to make change.

Why does the LGBT+ community need your support?

Like I mentioned before, the amount of oppression and rejection that these individuals face is not something we would like for a loved one, or just another human being to go through. “Greater levels of family rejection tend to be associated with greater levels of depression, substance abuse, unprotected sex, and suicide attempts in North American samples” (Tskhay). By being active educating other people, you could change someone’s mind and possibly help another individual going through a tough situation.
